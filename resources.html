Fellowships and Opportunities

General Fellowships

Microsoft Womens Scholarship [link]
Covers a stipend and conference travel allowance. For one academic year. Nominated by the university.
Susan Lipschutz, Margaret Ayers Host and Anna Olcott Smith awards [link]
For spring/summer research. Must have achieved candidacy and be nominated by your program.
Barbour Scholarship[link]
Citizens of countries in Asia (Including Turkey and everything east of there) who are not US citizens or permanent residents nor married to one, and intend to return to their native countries to pursue a professional career.
Scholarships for Women Studying Information Security[link]
Master's students only, preference to US citizens and permanent residents.

For attending conferences

ACM-W Scholarship [link]
Note that you are not required to be presenting a paper.
N2Women Fellowships[link]
Must help organize the N2Women meeting, check their website for the conference for which they are looking for applicants.

Organizations

GradSWE[link]
Graduate Committee of the Society of Women Engineers. Provides professional and social networking opportunities, organizes speakers, symposiums and presentations, encourages undergraduates to attend grad school, and disseminates information about a variety of events and opportunities that may be of interest.
GEECS[link]
Girls in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. Both undergraduate and graduate. Organizes a variety of social, professional and academic events, including outreach to high schools.
CSEG [link]
CSE Graduates: General graduate student society, representing all CSE grads. Also hold a variety of social events and programs to improve graduate life, including Chez Betty.
N2Women [link]
Networking Networking Women, an international organization for researchers in communications and networking research. Look out for their events at major networking conferences.
